Peak Climbing in Nepal

Nepal is the world’s top destination for peak climbing and Himalayan mountaineering, offering adventure seekers the chance to scale dramatic trekking peaks and Himalayan summits. With over 30 trekking peaks in Nepal open to climbers, the country provides a unique mix of high-altitude adventure, glacial landscapes, and Sherpa culture. Popular climbs like Island Peak (Imja Tse), Mera Peak, Lobuche East, and other Himalayan trekking peaks attract climbers from all over the globe. These expeditions traverse remote valleys, traditional Sherpa villages, and glacier-covered terrains, giving you an authentic Nepal mountaineering experience. Whether you are a first-time climber or a seasoned adventurer, Nepal’s peaks under 7,000 meters are ideal for Himalayan climbing training before attempting the world’s tallest mountains.

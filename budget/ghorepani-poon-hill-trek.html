Overview

The Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is a short yet rewarding trekking experience in the Annapurna region of Nepal, ideal for beginners and seasoned trekkers alike. This 7-day trek offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural exploration, and panoramic Himalayan views. The highlight of the trek is the sunrise from Poon Hill, where trekkers can witness the majestic Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges, including peaks like Mt. Dhaulagiri, Annapurna I, and Nilgiri.

The journey begins with a scenic drive from Pokhara to Nayapul, followed by trekking through picturesque villages such as Tikhedhunga, Ulleri, Ghorepani, and Ghandruk. Along the way, trekkers will experience the hospitality of local teahouses, enjoy traditional Nepali meals, and immerse themselves in the rich culture of the Gurung and Magar communities.

Trekkers need an Annapurna Conservation Area Permit (ACAP) and a Trekking Information Management System (TIMS) Card, which can be arranged by Above & Beyond Trekking. The trek is best undertaken during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons, when the weather is clear, and the views are at their most spectacular.

With its moderate difficulty level, the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is suitable for trekkers of all fitness levels. Hiring a professional guide and porter is recommended to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience. This trek is a must for anyone looking to explore the Himalayan landscapes and create unforgettable memories.

Highlights

  • Breathtaking panoramic views of Annapurna I (8,091m), Dhaulagiri, Machapuchare (Fishtail), and Hiunchuli
  • Unforgettable sunrise from Poon Hill lighting up snowcapped Himalayan peaks
  • Cultural immersion in Gurung and Magar villages like Ghorepani, Ghandruk, and Tadapani
  • Explore local monasteries, stone houses, and experience warm Nepali mountain hospitality
  • Trek through vibrant rhododendron forests, bamboo groves, and riverside trails—especially colorful in spring
  • Suitable for beginners and families—moderate difficulty with well-marked trails
  • Stay in cozy teahouses serving hearty local meals and drinks
  • Encounter Himalayan wildlife—langurs, Himalayan thars, and colorful bird species
  • Excellent photography opportunities of mountain vistas, village life, and forest landscapes

Itinerary

Day 1: Drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara

Drive Time: 6-7 hours

Optional Flight: 25 minutes (US$125)

Overnight: Hotel in Pokhara

Enjoy a scenic bus ride through beautiful mountain landscapes, rivers, and small villages. The journey offers spectacular views of the Himalayas. Upon arrival, relax and enjoy the evening in Pokhara, a peaceful lakeside town.

Day 2: Drive to Nayapul & Trek to Hille (1,475m / 4,838ft)

Drive Time: 1.5 hours from Pokhara

Trek Time: 3 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Hille

After a short flight to Pokhara, drive to Nayapul and begin your trek through the scenic Modi Khola and Bhurungdi River valleys. Reach the quiet village of Hille for an easy first day on the trail.

Day 3: Hille to Ghorepani Deurali (2,800m / 9,184ft)

Trek Time: 6–7 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Ghorepani

Ascend through Tikhedunga and up the famous Ulleri stone staircase. Hike through beautiful oak and rhododendron forests before reaching the stunning village of Ghorepani surrounded by mountain views.

Day 4: Poon Hill Sunrise & Trek to Tadapani (3,210m / 10,528ft, 2,600m / 8,528ft)

Sunrise Hike: 1 hour to Poon Hill

Trek Time: 6 hours to Tadapani

Overnight: Teahouse in Tadapani

Start early to witness a magical sunrise from Poon Hill with views of Dhaulagiri, Annapurna I & South. Continue through rhododendron forests and scenic ridges to Tadapani.

Day 5: Tadapani to Ghandruk (1,940m / 6,363ft)

Trek Time: 3 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Ghandruk

Descend through vibrant rhododendron and magnolia forests to the traditional Gurung village of Ghandruk. Explore local culture, visit the museum, and enjoy views of Annapurna South and Machapuchare.

Day 6: Ghandruk to Nayapul, Drive to Pokhara (822m / 2,696ft)

Trek Time: 3–4 hours

Drive Time: 1 hour

Overnight: Hotel in Pokhara

Hike down to Nayapul and return to Pokhara. Enjoy a relaxing afternoon exploring the lakeside, visiting the World Peace Pagoda or the Mountain Museum.

Day 7: Drive from Pokhara to Kathmandu (1,400m / 4,592ft)

Drive Time: 6–7 hours

Optional Flight: 25 minutes (US$125)

Return to Kathmandu by tourist bus or private transport. After checking in to your hotel, enjoy a farewell evening in the capital city.

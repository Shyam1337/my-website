Manaslu Circuit Trek Itinerary – Complete Guide for Trekkers

The Manaslu Circuit Trek typically takes 14 to 18 days, making it one of Nepal’s most scenic and remote trekking adventures. Starting with a drive from Kathmandu to Soti Khola, this off-the-beaten-path route circles the majestic Mount Manaslu (8,163m)—the eighth-highest peak in the world.

Trekkers will pass through traditional Gurung and Tibetan-influenced villages, cross suspension bridges over roaring rivers, and hike through pristine forests and alpine landscapes. A key highlight is the challenging crossing of Larkya La Pass (5,160 meters / 16,929 feet), which offers breathtaking views of peaks like Himlung, Cheo, and Annapurna II.

As a restricted area trek, the Manaslu Circuit offers a quieter, culturally immersive alternative to the Annapurna or Everest routes. You'll need special permits, and a licensed guide is mandatory. The route also passes through the Manaslu Conservation Area and touches the edge of the Annapurna region.

How long is the Manaslu Circuit Trek?

The Manaslu Circuit Trek typically spans 14 to 18 days, depending on acclimatization needs and optional side hikes. Most journeys begin and end in Arughat Bazaar or Soti Khola, taking you around the massive Mount Manaslu (8,163 m).

How difficult is the Manaslu Circuit Trek?

This is a challenging high-altitude trek that requires strong fitness, stamina, and experience with sustained elevation gains. The technical section is at Larkya La Pass (5,160 m), made tougher by altitude and weather.

Where does the Manaslu Circuit Trek start?

The trek typically begins at Arughat Bazaar or Soti Khola, after a 6–8 hour drive from Kathmandu through beautiful village settlements and terraced farmland.

Where is the Manaslu region?

The Manaslu region lies in north-central Nepal, bordering Tibet, within the Manaslu Conservation Area. It's a remote, culturally rich region with Gurung and Tibetan-influenced communities.

How to get to Soti Khola (Trek Starting Point)

You can reach Soti Khola or Arughat Bazaar by jeep or local bus from Kathmandu. Allow 6–8 hours of travel time over winding mountain roads.

Do you need a guide or porter for the Manaslu Trek?

Yes—a licensed guide is mandatory for the restricted Manaslu Circuit. Hiring a porter to carry 15–20 kg of gear is strongly recommended and will make the trek more comfortable.

Tips for altitude and acclimatization

Use gradual ascent, stay well-hydrated, and include rest days, particularly before and after Larkya La. Side trips to Pungyen Gompa or Lihi help with acclimatization.

Insurance for the Manaslu Circuit Trek

Ensure your travel insurance covers treks above 5,000 m, helicopter evacuation, high-altitude medical emergencies, and trip delays caused by weather or landslides.

What is the accommodation like on the Manaslu trek?

You will stay in basic local teahouses, mostly twin- or dorm-style rooms, with shared bathrooms and simple meals. Standard improves as you descend from higher camps like Samagaon and Dharamsala.

When is the best time to trek Manaslu Circuit?

The ideal trekking seasons are autumn (September–November) and spring (March–May), offering stable weather and views from Larkya La. Winter and monsoon should be avoided.

What permits are required for the Manaslu Trek?

You’ll need a Manaslu Conservation Area Permit (MCAP), a Trekker’s Information Management System (TIMS) card, and a restricted-area permit specific to the Manaslu region.

How to prepare for the Manaslu Circuit Trek?

Begin training 2–3 months before departure with endurance cardio, uphill hikes, and weight-carrying practice. Experience trekking at high altitude is a significant advantage.

Can I get a visa for Nepal on arrival?

Yes. You may obtain a Nepal tourist visa on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport or apply in advance through the Department of Immigration website.

Do I need previous trekking experience?

Previous high-altitude trekking experience is recommended for the Manaslu Circuit, but dedicated and well-prepared trekkers with guide support can also complete it successfully.

Manaslu Circuit Trek Itinerary

A standard itinerary: Soti Khola – Machha Khola – Jagat – Deng – Namrung – Samagaon – Pungyen Gompa – Larkya La – Bimtang – Dharapani – return to Kathmandu or Pokhara.

What to pack for the Manaslu Circuit Trek?

Key gear includes high-altitude sleeping bag, down jacket, waterproof layers, sturdy trekking boots, trekking poles, water purification, first-aid kit, headlamp, sun protection, and altitude medication.

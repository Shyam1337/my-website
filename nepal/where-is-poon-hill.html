Poon Hill Trek Itinerary – Complete Guide for Trekkers

The Poon Hill Trek typically takes 4 to 6 days, making it one of the best short treks in Nepal for beginners and families. Starting from Nayapul, just a short drive from Pokhara, the trail passes through charming Gurung and Magar villages, lush rhododendron forests, and scenic terraced farmlands.

Key highlights include an overnight stay in Ghorepani and an early morning hike to Poon Hill (3,210 meters / 10,531 feet), where you'll witness a breathtaking sunrise over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. You'll also pass through Ghandruk, a culturally rich village known for its hospitality and traditional architecture.

How long is the Poon Hill Trek?

The Poon Hill Trek usually takes 4 to 6 days, depending on your pace and chosen route. It’s ideal for trekkers with limited time who still want to experience Himalayan views, culture, and nature. Most routes start and end in Nayapul, near Pokhara.

How difficult is the Poon Hill Trek?

This is considered an easy to moderate trek, suitable for beginners and families. While there are daily ascents and descents, the altitudes are relatively low, peaking at 3,210 meters (10,531 feet) at Poon Hill.

Where does the Poon Hill Trek start?

The trek typically begins at Nayapul, about a 1.5 to 2-hour drive from Pokhara. Some itineraries may also start from Ulleri or Ghandruk depending on transportation and time.

Where is Poon Hill?

Poon Hill is located in the Annapurna region of central Nepal, above the village of Ghorepani. It offers one of the most famous sunrise viewpoints in the Himalayas, overlooking peaks like Machapuchare, Dhaulagiri, and Annapurna South.

How to Get to Nayapul (Gateway to Poon Hill)

You can reach Nayapul by private vehicle or local bus from Pokhara. The drive takes around 1.5 to 2 hours. Pokhara itself is accessible by domestic flight or tourist bus from Kathmandu.

Do You Need a Guide or Porter?

While the Poon Hill trail is well-marked and often busy, hiring a licensed guide enhances your experience with local insights. A porter can carry 15–20kg, making your trek more enjoyable, especially on the steep stone steps.

Tips for Altitude and Acclimatization

Altitude sickness is rare on the Poon Hill Trek, as the highest point is only 3,210 meters. Still, stay hydrated, ascend gradually, and rest if needed. No acclimatization days are typically required.

Insurance for the Poon Hill Trek

Though the altitude is moderate, it's recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical care, accidents, and emergency evacuation. Coverage up to 4,000m is sufficient for this trek.

What is the accommodation like on the trek?

You’ll stay in local teahouses offering twin rooms, shared bathrooms, and basic meals. Popular villages like Ghorepani and Ghandruk have better facilities and warm hospitality.

When is the best time to trek Poon Hill?

The best seasons are spring (March–May) for blooming rhododendrons and autumn (September–November) for clear mountain views. Winter (December–February) is also good if you’re prepared for colder weather.

Do I need a permit for the Poon Hill Trek?

Yes. You’ll need two permits: the Annapurna Conservation Area Permit (ACAP) and a TIMS card. These can be arranged in Kathmandu or Pokhara, or handled by your trekking company.

How to prepare for the Poon Hill Trek?

Although it’s a short trek, basic fitness helps. Train with cardio and leg strength workouts. Practice walking on stairs or hills with a light backpack to build endurance for the stone staircases.

Can I get a visa for Nepal on arrival?

Yes. You can get a tourist visa on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu or apply online before your trip through Nepal's Department of Immigration.

Do I need previous trekking experience?

No experience is needed. The Poon Hill Trek is beginner-friendly and perfect for first-time trekkers looking to enjoy the Himalayas without extreme altitude or long durations.

Poon Hill Trek Itinerary

A classic itinerary includes: Nayapul – Tikhedhunga – Ulleri – Ghorepani – Poon Hill (sunrise) – Tadapani – Ghandruk – Nayapul. The route can be customized depending on your pace and starting point.

What to pack for the Poon Hill Trek?

Essentials include: warm layers, rain gear, good hiking shoes, headlamp, refillable water bottle, sunscreen, snacks, personal meds, and a small daypack. No need for mountaineering gear.

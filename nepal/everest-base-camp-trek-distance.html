Everest Base Camp Trek Distance

The Everest Base Camp Trek covers a total distance of about 130 km, usually completed in 12 to 14 days. In this guide, we break down the route step by step—showing daily walking distances, highlights along the trail, and what to expect as you journey to the base of the world’s highest peak.

How Long Is the Everest Base Camp Trek?

Most trekkers complete Everest Base Camp (EBC) in 12–14 days, covering around 130 km with two acclimatization days. Below, find a practical day‑by‑day plan, distance and elevation details, plus tips to go slower or faster safely.

Typical Duration: 12–14 Days

The classic Everest Base Camp trek generally takes 12–14 days door‑to‑door from Kathmandu. This includes flying to Lukla, trekking to Base Camp, an optional sunrise hike to Kala Patthar, and returning to Lukla for your flight back.

Why 12–14 days? The extra time is for acclimatization, which helps your body adapt to altitude and keeps the trek safe and enjoyable.

Total Distance & Daily Hiking Hours

Expect around 130 km (round‑trip) on the classic Lukla–EBC–Lukla route. Most days involve 5–7 hours of steady hiking with breaks, while acclimatization days include shorter “active rest” hikes.

Elevation & Acclimatization

  • Highest sleeping point: Gorakshep (~5,164 m)
  • Everest Base Camp: ~5,364 m
  • Kala Patthar (optional): ~5,545 m (best sunrise views)

Two acclimatization nights—usually in Namche Bazaar (~3,440 m) and Dingboche (~4,410 m)—significantly reduce the risk of altitude sickness. On these days, you’ll take a shorter hike high and return to sleep lower (“climb high, sleep low”).

Everest Base Camp Trek Itinerary

Here’s a realistic schedule many trekkers follow. Distances and times are approximate and vary with conditions.

Day Route Distance (km) Hours Sleep Altitude
1 Kathmandu → Fly Lukla (2,860 m), trek to Phakding 8–9 3–4 ~2,610 m
2 Phakding → Namche Bazaar 10–12 5–6 ~3,440 m
3 Acclimatization in Namche (hike to ~3,900 m) 3-5 3-4 ~3,440 m
4 Namche → Tengboche 9-10 5–6 ~3,860 m
5 Tengboche → Dingboche 10-11 5–6 ~4,410 m
6 Acclimatization in Dingboche (hike to ~4,900–5,000 m) 3-5 3-4 ~4,410 m
7 Dingboche → Lobuche 8-9 5–6 ~4,940 m
8 Lobuche → Gorakshep → Everest Base Camp → Gorakshep 4-5 3-4 ~5,164 m
9 Gorakshep → (Kala Patthar optional) → Pheriche 12–14 7-8 ~4,250 m
10 Pheriche → Namche 14–15 7-8 ~3,440 m
11 Namche → Lukla 18–19 7–8 ~2,860 m
12 Fly Lukla → Kathmandu — — ~1,400 m

Have more time? Stretch to 13–14 days with a gentler pace, extra acclimatization, or side trips (e.g., Chukhung Ri or Ama Dablam Base Camp).

What Can Change the Duration?

  • Weather & Flights: Lukla flights can be delayed—build in buffer days.
  • Acclimatization Needs: Extra rest days may be added based on how you feel at altitude.
  • Side Trips: Kala Patthar, Ama Dablam BC, or Chukhung Ri add time but reward you with views.
  • Trail Conditions: Snow, ice, and seasonal crowds can affect pace.
  • Fitness & Pack Weight: Train beforehand and keep your daypack light.

Tips to Trek Safely & Comfortably

  1. Follow acclimatization best practices: “Climb high, sleep low,” hydrate well, and report symptoms early.
  2. Train 6–8 weeks in advance: Focus on hiking endurance and stair climbing.
  3. Pack smart: See our EBC trek packing list and keep total weight reasonable.
  4. Choose the right season: Peak windows are spring (Mar–May) and autumn (Sep–Nov).
  5. Hire experienced guides & porters: Local expertise boosts safety, logistics, and enjoyment.

Mahare Danda Trek Overview

The Mohare Danda Trek is a short and rewarding trekking experience in the Annapurna region of Nepal. Known for its breathtaking mountain scenery and rich cultural encounters, this route provides a quieter alternative to the more crowded trails while offering equally stunning views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges.

This community-based trek takes you through lush rhododendron forests, traditional Gurung and Magar villages, and terraced farmlands, allowing you to immerse yourself in the daily life of the local people. Along the way, you will experience eco-friendly lodges, organic farming practices, and warm hospitality that highlight Nepal’s commitment to sustainable tourism.

One of the highlights of the trek is reaching Mohare Danda, a panoramic viewpoint at 3,300 meters, where trekkers are rewarded with spectacular sunrise and sunset vistas over some of the world’s highest peaks. The peaceful trails and pristine landscapes make it an ideal choice for nature lovers and those seeking a cultural adventure.

The Mohare Danda Trek is suitable for both beginners and experienced trekkers looking for a short trek in the Annapurna region. With a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and sustainable travel practices, it offers a memorable Himalayan journey away from the crowds.

Mahare Danda Trek Highlights

  • Panoramic Himalayan views of Annapurna I (8,091m), Machapuchare (Fishtail), Dhaulagiri, and Hiunchuli
  • Witness golden sunrise and sunset lighting up the snowcapped peaks from Mohare Danda and nearby ridges
  • Stay in eco-lodges operated by local communities, promoting sustainable tourism
  • Rich cultural experience with Gurung, Magar, and Tibetan communities
  • Visit traditional villages like Nangi, explore local monasteries, and enjoy warm mountain hospitality
  • Learn about organic farming and eco-projects in Nangi Village
  • Trek through diverse terrain—rhododendron forests, bamboo groves, rivers, and terraced hills
  • Off-the-beaten-path experience with peaceful, less-crowded trails
  • Moderate difficulty—perfect for beginners, families, and trekkers with average fitness levels
  • Spot local wildlife like langurs, Himalayan thar, and vibrant bird species
  • Vibrant rhododendron blooms in spring add bursts of color to the trail
  • Incredible photographic opportunities of Himalayan landscapes, village life, and forest scenery

Mahare Danda Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m / 4,592ft)

Overnight: Hotel in Kathmandu

Welcome to Nepal! Our team will meet you at Tribhuvan International Airport and transfer you to your hotel in Kathmandu. You can explore the vibrant streets of Thamel or rest after your flight.

Day 2: Drive/Fly to Pokhara (822m / 2,696ft)

Flight Time: 25 minutes

Drive Time: 6–7 hours

Overnight: Hotel in Pokhara

We either take a short flight or a scenic road trip to Pokhara. Spend the evening enjoying Phewa Lake, the World Peace Pagoda, and Lakeside cafes.

Day 3: Drive to Galeshwor & Trek to Bans Kharka (1,525m / 5,003ft)

Drive Time: 3–4 hours

Trek Time: 3–4 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Bans Kharka

A scenic drive brings us to Galeshwor, the trek’s starting point. We cross the Kali Gandaki River and hike through forests, farmlands, and small villages before reaching Bans Kharka.

Day 4: Trek from Bans Kharka to Nangi Village (2,260m / 7,415ft)

Trek Time: 5–6 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Nangi Village

Trek through lush rhododendron forests and traditional Magar villages. We’ll reach Nangi Village, known for its eco-lodges, organic farming, and local schools.

Day 5: Trek from Nangi Village to Mohare Danda/Phulbari (3,300m / 10,827ft)

Trek Time: 6–7 hours

Overnight: Teahouse at Mohare Danda

Ascend to the summit of Mohare Danda, a beautiful vantage point with panoramic views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges. Expect unforgettable sunrise and sunset vistas.

Day 6: Trek from Mohare Danda to Tikot Village (2,210m / 7,250ft)

Trek Time: 5–6 hours

Overnight: Teahouse in Tikot Village

Descend through forests and terraced fields to Tikot Village, a charming Magar settlement where we’ll experience authentic local culture and warm hospitality.

Day 7: Trek to Tiplyang & Drive to Pokhara

Trek Time: 3 hours

Drive Time: 4–5 hours

Overnight: Hotel in Pokhara

We descend to Tiplyang and drive back to Pokhara. The evening is free for lakeside relaxation or exploring the local eateries and shops.

Day 8: Drive/Fly Back to Kathmandu

Flight Time: 25 minutes

Drive Time: 6–7 hours

Overnight: Hotel in Kathmandu

Take a short flight or scenic drive back to Kathmandu. In the evening, enjoy a farewell dinner featuring live cultural performances.

Day 9: Departure from Kathmandu

Transfer: Tribhuvan International Airport

After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your international flight home. We hope you had a memorable trek and look forward to welcoming you again!
